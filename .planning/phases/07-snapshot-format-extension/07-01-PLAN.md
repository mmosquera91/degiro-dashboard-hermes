<objective>
Configure Docker named volume `brokr_snapshots` mounted at `/data/snapshots` in docker-compose.yml. Delivers DOCK-01 and DOCK-02.
</objective>

<context>
@docker-compose.yml
</context>

<tasks>

<task type="auto">
  <name>Task 1: Add named volume declaration and service mount</name>
  <files>docker-compose.yml</files>
  <read_first>docker-compose.yml</read_first>
  <action>
Add a top-level `volumes` section and a volume mount in the brokr service.

1. Add `volumes:` section at the end of docker-compose.yml (bottom of file, after the healthcheck block):
```yaml
volumes:
  brokr_snapshots:
```

2. In the brokr service, add `brokr_snapshots:/data/snapshots` to the volumes list (after the existing `env_file` line):
```yaml
    volumes:
      - brokr_snapshots:/data/snapshots
```

The final docker-compose.yml must have:
- A `volumes:` top-level key with `brokr_snapshots:` declaration (Docker creates this automatically at `/var/lib/docker/volumes/brokr_snapshots/_data`)
- The brokr service must have `volumes: - brokr_snapshots:/data/snapshots` in its volumes list

Do NOT use a bind mount (`./snapshots:/data/snapshots`) — use named volume only.
  </action>
  <verify>
    <automated>grep -q "brokr_snapshots" docker-compose.yml && grep -q "brokr_snapshots:/data/snapshots" docker-compose.yml && echo "PASS" || echo "FAIL"</automated>
  </verify>
  <done>docker-compose.yml has named volume `brokr_snapshots` mounted at `/data/snapshots`</done>
  <acceptance_criteria>
    - docker-compose.yml contains `brokr_snapshots:` (top-level volume declaration)
    - docker-compose.yml contains `brokr_snapshots:/data/snapshots` (service volume mount)
    - docker-compose.yml does NOT contain `./snapshots:/data/snapshots` (no bind mount)
    - yaml syntax is valid: `python -c "import yaml; yaml.safe_load(open('docker-compose.yml'))"` passes
  </acceptance_criteria>
</task>
